# Go-To-Market: AgreementMitra — Hyderabad, B2C, ₹25k/month

**Scope:** Launch the self-serve (no-login) Aadhaar-eSign rental-agreement MVP
direct to tenants & landlords in **one anchor city — Hyderabad** — on a hard
**₹25,000/month** marketing ceiling (~US$300). Goal: capital-efficient growth
with strict CAC discipline.

> Confidence tags: **[FACT]** = well-supported, often a primary source.
> **[EST]** = informed estimate / soft benchmark (treat as order-of-magnitude).
> **[FLAG]** = a claim found circulating that is wrong or unverified — do not
> repeat it in marketing.
>
> Source method: ~20 sources fetched, claims adversarially verified (3-vote).
> The benchmark numbers below survived as *directional* but most ad-cost figures
> trace to marketing-agency blogs, not primary data — hence **[EST]**.

---

## TL;DR — the honest answer

At ₹25k/month you **cannot buy your way in**. The well-funded incumbent
(NoBroker) already does Aadhaar-eSign rental agreements in Hyderabad from ₹399
with 4,500 reviews and bank discounts **[FACT]**. So the plan is **not** "ads
vs. influencers vs. ground game" — it's:

1. **Win intent you don't pay for** — SEO + Google Business Profile, Telangana-
   specific content, Quora/Reddit/FB-group answers. *(Engine. ~free, slow.)*
2. **Own the high-rise moment of need** via **low/no-cash RWA partnerships**, not
   paid society-app ads (those are out of budget — see below). *(Your edge.)*
3. **Spend the small cash slice surgically** — a thin Google Search test on the
   highest-intent keywords + a double-sided WhatsApp referral loop. *(Accelerant.)*

**MyGate/ADDA paid advertising is off the table:** MyGate's ad minimum is cited
at **~₹50,000+** (up to ₹5,00,000) — *more than your entire monthly budget* —
and that figure comes only from third-party ad-resellers, so even it is soft
**[EST/FLAG]**. Treat society apps as a *partnership/organic* channel, not a paid one.

## 1. Channel strategy at ₹25k/month

### Google Search Ads — small, surgical, high-intent only
- India Google CPCs are **70–85% lower than the US** **[EST]**. Generic clicks
  run **₹5–₹50**; real-estate / legal / finance *intent* keywords run higher —
  roughly **₹30–₹150** **[EST]**. (Exact per-vertical numbers circulating online
  are agency-blog data and conflict by up to an order of magnitude — **[FLAG]**;
  don't trust a specific "₹38 for legal" figure.)
- Practical floor for a meaningful campaign in India: **₹5,000–₹10,000/month**
  (₹200–₹500/day) **[EST]**.
- **Recommendation:** run **₹6,000–₹8,000/month** on a *tight* exact/phrase list:
  `rental agreement hyderabad`, `rent agreement online hyderabad`,
  `e-stamp paper hyderabad`, `11 month rental agreement online`. Geo-fence to
  Hyderabad. Send to a fast, trust-heavy landing page. Kill any keyword with CPL
  above your target. **Don't run broad match.**
- At ~₹100 CPL-target discipline this is a few hundred clicks → expect only a
  **handful to low-tens of conversions/month** **[EST]**. This is a *learning &
  intent-capture* budget, not a growth engine.

### Meta (Instagram/Facebook) — retargeting only, skip cold prospecting
- Meta in India: technical min ~₹100/day; **lead-gen needs ₹300–₹500/day to be
  meaningful** **[EST]** — that alone would eat ~₹9–15k/month. Not worth it cold
  at this budget against an unknown brand.
- **Recommendation:** spend **₹0–₹2,000/month** on **retargeting only** (people
  who hit the site but didn't finish). Reels carry the lowest CPMs **[EST]**.
  Cheap, warm, high-converting. Do **not** run cold awareness campaigns yet.

### Influencers — barter / nano-tier only
- Nano (1K–10K followers) Reels: lifestyle **₹1,000–₹3,000**; finance/tech nanos
  **₹8,000–₹12,000** **[EST]**. Micro (10K–50K) jumps to **₹1,857–₹34,834/Reel**
  **[EST/FLAG]** (single rate-card source — negotiate, don't anchor on it).
- **Recommendation:** **don't pay cash.** Offer **free agreements + a rev-share/
  affiliate code** to 3–5 Hyderabad-niche nanos: "moving to Hyderabad", local
  city pages, PG/flat-hunting, young-professional finance. Budget **₹0–₹3,000**
  for one paid nano test only if a barter partner over-performs.

### SEO + content — THE long-term engine (start day 1)
- Timeline: **3–6 months to measurable results; 6–12 in competitive metros**
  **[FACT/EST]**. Start now precisely because it's slow.
- **Google Business Profile is the fast local win** — can rank in Maps within
  *weeks*; target 4.0+ rating and 50+ reviews **[EST]**. Set this up week 1.
- **Long-tail Telangana queries convert best** **[EST]** and incumbents under-
  serve them. Write genuinely correct, up-to-date pages:
  - "11-month vs registered rental agreement in Telangana — which do I need?"
  - "Rental agreement stamp duty & registration in Hyderabad (2026)"
  - "Is an Aadhaar-eSigned rental agreement valid in court? (India)"
  - "How to make a rent agreement online in Hyderabad without a notary"
- This doubles as your **trust content** (see §3) and your **ad landing pages**.

### WhatsApp / referral loop
- **Double-sided incentives** (e.g. ₹100 referrer + ₹100 referee) beat single-
  sided by **40–60%** on K-factor in India **[EST]**; **WhatsApp is the primary
  share channel** (not email) **[FACT-ish/EST]**; pay out only **after the
  referee's first completed agreement** to keep fraud <2% **[EST]**.
- **Recommendation:** build a WhatsApp-first referral with post-purchase payout.
  Budget it as **variable CAC (~₹100–₹200/referred customer)**, not fixed spend —
  it only costs money when it works.

## 2. The high-rise ground game (your main idea — refined)

The instinct is right: a >1000-unit complex has constant tenant churn, so each
move-in is a fresh agreement need. But **the channel is partnership, not paid ads.**

- **MyGate scale is real** — 5M+ homes, 27k societies, ~80% gated-community share,
  pincode/society-level targeting **[FACT]**. **But the paid ad minimum is
  ~₹50,000+** **[EST/FLAG]** — over your whole budget. **ADDA** is similar.
  ⇒ Use these as *organic/partnership surfaces*, don't buy ads.
- **What to do instead (low/no cash):**
  - **Adopt 3–5 anchor societies** in Gachibowli / Kondapur / Narsingi / Kokapet /
    Manikonda / Miyapur. Win the **RWA / management committee** with a
    *free-for-the-committee + small rev-share or referral fee* deal, or "free
    agreements for the first N residents."
  - Get into the **apartment WhatsApp groups** and **notice boards** (admin-
    permitted), and the **move-in/onboarding moment** (security desk / facility
    manager hands new tenants your QR).
  - **Facility managers & local brokers** as referrers on a per-deal fee — they
    sit at the exact point of need.
- **Low intro price per society:** viable *as a wedge*, but protect unit
  economics — a one-time per-agreement product can't subsidize forever. Prefer
  "**free for the committee / first movers, normal price after**" over blanket
  discounting. Measure agreements-per-society before scaling to society #6+.

**Why this beats ads at ₹25k:** zero media cost, high trust (RWA endorsement),
and it concentrates word-of-mouth inside a dense 1000+ household graph — exactly
where a referral loop compounds.

## 3. Positioning & trust (the real conversion bottleneck)

Indian buyers are skeptical of online legal docs. Awareness isn't the problem —
**trust is.** Lead with legality and correctness, not price.

**Trust signals to put on every page (all [FACT]):**
- Aadhaar eSign is legally valid under **Section 3A + Schedule II, IT Act 2000**
  (inserted by **Gazette GSR 61(E), 28 Jan 2015**); electronic signatures carry
  legal equivalence to wet-ink (**Sec 5, IT Act**).
- eSigned docs are **enforceable in court** via electronic-evidence rules
  (Section 65B / now BSA 2023), with statutory presumptions favoring a secure
  signature.
- **Rental/lease agreements are NOT in the excluded list** for Aadhaar eSign
  (the exclusions are negotiable instruments, PoA, trusts, wills, and sale/
  conveyance of immovable property).
- Telangana supports **online e-stamping** (Registration & Stamps Dept eSTAMPS;
  **SHCIL** authorized for verification) — you can deliver a properly stamped,
  legally usable agreement digitally.

**[FLAG] — do NOT over-claim:**
- Don't say Aadhaar eSign is "*stronger than / exceeds* wet-ink." That's vendor
  marketing overreach; the law gives *equivalence + presumptions*, not superiority.
- Don't cite a blanket "all rental agreements must be digitally stamped from
  1 July 2025, ₹5,000 penalty" rule — that circulating claim is **unverified and
  not tied to any Telangana statute** **[FLAG]**.

**Differentiators vs. cheap competitors:** *correctness* (right stamp duty / right
11-vs-12-month guidance — consumers are visibly confused about Telangana rates),
*vernacular/Telugu* support, *no-login self-serve* simplicity, and *data privacy*
(you're handling Aadhaar-linked flows — say how you protect PII).

**Top objections to pre-empt:** "Is this valid in court?" · "Do I need
registration / will the Sub-Registrar accept it?" · "Is my Aadhaar/OTP safe?" ·
"Why not just go to the stamp vendor I know?"

## 5. Regulatory constraints on marketing claims

- **11-month agreements avoid mandatory registration** in Telangana; registration
  becomes compulsory for leases **exceeding one year (12 months)** — Registration
  Act 1908 S.17(1)(d) **[FACT]**. *This is why 11-month is the standard product.*
  - **[FLAG]** Several sites phrase it as "more than 11 months = compulsory
    registration." That's imprecise. Use the **12-month / one-year** threshold.
- **Stamp duty rate: get it from the primary source, don't quote blogs.** Blog/
  competitor figures (0.4% vs 0.5% vs tiered "0.4% for 0–10 yrs…") **conflict and
  several are contradicted by the official schedule** **[FLAG]**. Authority =
  **Telangana Registration & Stamps Dept official StampFees / Article 31 (LEASE)**
  (`registration.telangana.gov.in`). Compute from there in-product; cite it.
- **You can say:** "legally valid," "Aadhaar eSign recognized under the IT Act,"
  "properly e-stamped per Telangana rules," "court-admissible electronic record."
- **Avoid:** "registered" (unless you actually register at the Sub-Registrar),
  "stronger than wet-ink," any invented penalty/mandate, and exact stamp-duty
  percentages in ad copy (compute live; rates change).
- **Repo guardrail reminder:** sandbox + dummy data only; never log Aadhaar/OTP/
  VID/PII (already in CLAUDE.md — marketing demos must respect it).

## 6. 90-day bootstrap plan (≤ ₹25,000/month)

### Monthly budget allocation (~₹22.5k base, ₹2.5k buffer)
| Line | ₹/month | Why |
|---|---:|---|
| Google Search (Hyderabad, exact/phrase) | 7,000 | Only paid intent capture worth doing |
| Meta **retargeting** only | 2,000 | Cheap, warm, converts |
| Referral payouts (variable, double-sided) | 5,000 | Pay only on success; scales with traction |
| RWA/society activation (printing, QR standees, small committee perks) | 4,000 | Ground-game enablement |
| Nano-influencer/content (mostly barter; 1 paid test) | 2,500 | Reach without cash burn |
| Tools (SEO/keyword, landing builder, WhatsApp API tier) | 2,000 | Engine upkeep |
| **Buffer / experiment reserve** | 2,500 | Double-down on what works |
| **Total** | **25,000** | |

### Phasing
- **Month 1 — foundations & cheap tests.** Ship trust-heavy landing pages + the
  4 SEO articles. Set up **Google Business Profile** and start collecting reviews.
  Launch the ₹7k Google Search test. Sign **1–2 anchor societies** (free-for-
  committee). Stand up the WhatsApp referral loop. *Spend the buffer on nothing
  yet — gather data.*
- **Month 2 — find what converts.** Cut losing keywords; scale winners within the
  ₹7k. Turn on Meta retargeting. Onboard 3–5 nano/barter creators. Expand to
  **3–5 societies**; measure **agreements per society**. Push referral sharing.
- **Month 3 — concentrate spend.** Pour the buffer into the single best channel
  (likely Search winners or the best society's referral graph). Decide go/no-go on
  a 6th+ society. Document CAC by channel.

### Metrics to track
- **CAC by channel** (target blended **< ₹250–₹400**; flag any channel > ₹500).
- **Search:** CPC, CPL, keyword-level conversion → cost per completed agreement.
- **SEO:** GBP rank + reviews, article impressions/clicks, first top-10 keywords.
- **Society:** agreements per society per month, RWA referral conversion.
- **Referral:** **K-factor** (aim toward >1; 0.7 is typical, >1 excellent), share
  rate, fraud rate (<2%).
- **Funnel:** landing → start → OTP-eSign **completion rate** (your trust proof).

### Trade-offs of ₹25k & when to raise it
- You're buying **learning, not scale.** Volume will be modest; that's expected.
- **Raise the budget when:** a channel shows **repeatable CAC < your gross margin
  per agreement** AND **K-factor approaches/exceeds 1** AND at least one society
  hits steady repeat volume. Then the obvious scale step is **MyGate/ADDA paid
  placements** (now affordable) and paid mid-tier influencers — *not before.*
